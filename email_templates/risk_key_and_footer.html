Water Temperature Fishing Risk Level Key
Low (Under 65°) Water temperature is generally safe for fishing.
Moderate (65°-70°) Water temps are approaching unhealthy levels. Monitor temps and if still rising, don't play/fight fish and consider calling it a day.
High (Above 70°) Water temperature is dangerous for fish health and survival, avoid fishing until temperatures subside.

During seasonally warm water episodes, remember these tips:

  • Start early, finish early   Fish early in the day. Avoid peak afternoon heat. Monitor temps locally and call it when water temps climb towards and over 70 deg.
  • Land fish quickly   Use thick enough tippet to quickly bring in fish. Do not play or fight fish to exhaustion.
  • Keep them wet  Minimize time out of water. Unhook and release them without even removing from the water if possible.
  • Move higher  Fish cooler high-elevation headwaters until the hot weather breaks.

Information on fish health and temperature risk levels was developed with input from Colorado Parks and Wildlife (CPW) and the Water Quality Control Division.
